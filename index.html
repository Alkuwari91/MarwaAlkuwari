Summary of Unit 1: Introduction to Machine Learning (ML)

Introduction to Machine Learning: Machine learning (ML) plays a pivotal role in various industries, influencing everyday life, from web browsing to making critical decisions, such as bank loan approvals. With the rise of big data, machine learning algorithms can learn in real-time, empowering systems to make decisions autonomously with increased accuracy (Schwab & Zahid, 2020).

Key Topics:

In this unit, we explore the evolution of machine learning, discussing its past, present, and future. We also examine the challenges and opportunities of using algorithms, as well as the interplay between big data, machine learning, and artificial intelligence (AI). The availability of large datasets has enabled AI and ML to provide insightful analytics that drive innovation in various sectors, such as finance, healthcare, and manufacturing (World Economic Forum, 2023).

Learning Outcomes:

By the end of this unit, learners will have developed an understanding of the role of machine learning in future industries, identified the essential skills required to excel in the field, and gained insights into the challenges of implementing machine learning algorithms. Additionally, learners will be equipped to recognize the pitfalls associated with machine learning and how to address them effectively (Pant, 2019).

Assessment and Collaboration:

Throughout this unit, learners participate in collaborative discussions and group projects. Team dynamics and role allocation are emphasized, and guidelines for group work can be found on the department website. The e-Portfolio serves as a critical component of the final assessment, allowing students to demonstrate their learning through reflection and evidence-based contributions (Crawford, 2006; Zhao & Li, 2018).

Tools and Resources:

Learners are encouraged to use Google Colab, a browser-based tool that supports machine learning tasks using TensorFlow and neural networks. This tool is particularly useful for completing the tutorials and assignments throughout the course. Additionally, participation in the collaborative forums is vital for deeper engagement with peers and tutors (Kugler, 2022).

Unit 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Welcome to Week 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is crucial in data science and AI projects because it allows us to ensure that the future results will be valid, accurately interpreted, and applicable to business contexts. Certainty in results can only be achieved after raw data is validated and checked for anomalies, ensuring the dataset was collected correctly without errors. EDA also uncovers insights that may not have been initially obvious but can be informative to both business stakeholders and data scientists.

EDA is performed to define and refine the selection of feature variables that will be used in machine learning. Once familiar with the dataset, data scientists may return to the feature engineering step as initial features may not serve their intended purpose. Once the EDA process is complete, data scientists have a firm feature set needed for supervised or unsupervised machine learning.

This week we will cover:

  • The steps involved in EDA.
  • Understanding the dataset through feature exploration.
  • Spotting anomalies within the dataset.
  • Performing visual analysis of the dataset.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Undertake basic Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
  • Understand the dataset's structure and key features.
  • Prepare the dataset for machine learning.

Unit 3: Correlation and Regression

Welcome to Week 3. Correlation and regression are powerful statistical techniques used to quantify and describe the relationship between variables. Correlation measures the strength and type of relationship between variables, while regression expresses the relationship in the form of an equation that allows us to predict values for unseen variables.

This week we will cover:

  • The core aspects of correlation and regression.
  • Techniques to measure correlation and regression.
  • Practical applications with examples.

Learning Outcomes:

  • Gain a theoretical understanding of correlation and regression.
  • Understand how to compute correlation and regression.
  • Apply these statistical techniques to real-world scenarios.

Formative Activities:
