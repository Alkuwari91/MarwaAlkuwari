Unit 2: Introducing First Order Logic

This unit introduced the fundamentals of First Order Logic (FOL), a formal system used in AI to represent structured knowledge and reason logically. The seminar explored how FOL maps natural language into precise logical expressions using predicates, quantifiers, and connectives.

Key Learning

  • Applied cross-disciplinary AI concepts to real-world industrial sectors.
  • Understood how emerging technologies enhance operational efficiency.
  • Evaluated how these intelligent systems perform in practice and the issues they address.

Collaborative Discussion 3: Deep Learning – Summary Post

Summary by Marwa Alkuwari: This summary emphasized the ethical challenges linked to the rise of deep learning applications like DALL·E and ChatGPT. The discussion focused on key issues including misinformation and deepfakes, which risk distorting reality and causing harm. The conversation highlighted a shared call for improved transparency, detection, and ethical oversight.

We also examined intellectual property concerns due to AI training datasets containing copyrighted content. This raises unresolved questions about authorship and ownership, necessitating the development of clear legal standards. The risk of job displacement in creative fields was another focus, with both posts emphasizing the importance of preserving human creative roles alongside AI advancement.

Finally, concerns over privacy violations from impersonation technologies reinforced the need for regulatory safeguards. Overall, the discussion concluded that while deep learning brings transformative potential, responsible governance is essential to maximize benefits and minimize harm.

Final Reflection

This module on Intelligent Agents has been a transformative learning experience that allowed me to engage deeply with both theoretical foundations and practical applications of artificial intelligence. From the early exploration of agent-based computing and logic-based systems to more advanced topics such as deep learning and adaptive algorithms, each week brought new insights and challenges that broadened my understanding of intelligent systems.

I particularly valued the emphasis on real-world relevance—whether through discussions on agent communication protocols like KQML and KIF, or the ethical complexities of deepfake technologies and AI-generated content. These lessons deepened my critical thinking and highlighted the social responsibilities we must uphold as developers and researchers in this field.

The team project further amplified this learning by enabling collaborative problem-solving. Our multi-agent fitness monitoring system bridged theoretical knowledge with tangible implementation, reinforcing skills in system architecture, security protocols, and agile development. Working with my peers not only honed my technical and project management skills but also highlighted the importance of effective communication, mutual respect, and adaptability in a team setting.

Through reflective practice, I’ve become more aware of the competencies I’ve developed: analytical thinking, ethical reasoning, technical fluency, and collaboration. I feel better equipped to apply these skills in academic, professional, and real-world AI scenarios. Moving forward, I aim to continue exploring emerging AI trends, contribute to ethically responsible innovation, and pursue further interdisciplinary research opportunities.
